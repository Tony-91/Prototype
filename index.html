About Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes beaches, a harbor, tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior with an active volcano. The population is about 20,000, and the economy was traditionally fishing and agriculture until tourism boomed.

Frequently Requested Information

  • Power outlets are 120 volts (U.S. standard).
  • Alcohol cannot be sold between midnight and 9 a.m.
  • The drinking age is 18 but loosely enforced.
  • English is widely spoken by younger residents; less common in rural areas.
  • The island has one hospital and several multilingual clinics.
  • Violent crime is rare, though petty theft is increasing.
  • Many businesses close during national holidays.
  • USD is the official currency, but euros and yen are widely accepted.
  • Banks offer currency exchange and major credit cards are accepted.
